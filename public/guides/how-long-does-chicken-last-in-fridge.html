How Long Does Chicken Last in the Fridge?

📅 Updated July 2026⏱️ 6 min read✅ Fact-checked
Quick Answer

Raw chicken lasts 1–2 days in the fridge. Cooked chicken lasts 3–4 days. Raw chicken can be frozen for 9 months (pieces) or 12 months (whole). Signs of spoiled chicken include a sour smell, slimy texture, and grayish color. Always store raw chicken on the bottom shelf at 40°F (4°C) or below.

How Long Does Raw Chicken Last in the Fridge?

Raw chicken — whether whole, breasts, thighs, or ground — lasts only 1 to 2 days in the refrigerator at 40°F (4°C) or below. This short window makes chicken one of the most perishable proteins in your kitchen. If you're not going to cook it within 48 hours of purchase, freeze it immediately.

The USDA recommends cooking or freezing raw poultry within 1–2 days of purchase. This timeline starts from the purchase date, not the "sell by" date. If you bought chicken on Monday, plan to cook it by Tuesday or Wednesday at the latest.

How Long Does Cooked Chicken Last?

Cooked chicken lasts 3 to 4 days in the refrigerator. The clock starts the moment the chicken reaches a safe internal temperature of 165°F (74°C), not when you put it in the fridge. To maximize shelf life:

  • Cool cooked chicken to room temperature within 2 hours of cooking
  • Store in a shallow, airtight container (deep containers take too long to cool)
  • Label with the cooking date so you know when to use it by
  • Reheat to 165°F (74°C) internal temperature before eating

5 Signs Your Chicken Has Gone Bad

  1. Sour or sulfurous smell: Fresh raw chicken has almost no odor. If it smells sour, ammonia-like, or like rotten eggs, it's spoiled. This is the most reliable indicator.
  2. Slimy or sticky texture: Touch the chicken with clean fingers. Fresh chicken is moist but not slimy. A slick, sticky film indicates bacterial growth.
  3. Gray or greenish color: Fresh raw chicken is pink. If it has turned gray, dull, or has a greenish tinge, it's past its prime.
  4. Mold: Any visible mold (white, green, or black spots) means the chicken must be discarded immediately.
  5. Swollen or leaking packaging: If the plastic wrap is puffed up or there's excessive liquid, gas-producing bacteria are active.

Safe Chicken Storage: The Bottom-Shelf Rule

Always store raw chicken on the bottom shelf of your refrigerator. This is critical for food safety — raw chicken can contain Salmonella, Campylobacter, and other harmful bacteria. If raw chicken juice drips onto ready-to-eat foods below, it can cause cross-contamination and food poisoning.

Place raw chicken on a rimmed plate or tray to catch any drips, and keep it in its original packaging until ready to cook. Never wash raw chicken — splashing water spreads bacteria up to 3 feet around your sink.

How to Freeze Chicken Properly

Freezing is the best way to extend chicken's shelf life from days to months. Here's how to do it right:

  1. Wrap tightly: Remove from store packaging. Wrap each piece individually in plastic wrap, then place in a freezer bag. Squeeze out as much air as possible to prevent freezer burn.
  2. Label and date: Write the date and contents on the bag. Frozen chicken is safe indefinitely at 0°F, but quality degrades after 9–12 months.
  3. Portion before freezing: Freeze in meal-sized portions so you only thaw what you need. Refreezing thawed chicken degrades quality.
  4. Thaw safely: Thaw in the refrigerator (24 hours per 5 lbs), in cold water (change every 30 minutes), or in the microwave (cook immediately). Never thaw at room temperature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to cook chicken that's been in the fridge for 3 days?

It depends. Raw chicken should be cooked within 1–2 days of purchase. On day 3, check for signs of spoilage (smell, texture, color). If it passes all checks and was continuously refrigerated at 40°F or below, cooking it thoroughly to 165°F should kill most bacteria. However, if there's any doubt, throw it out — the risk of food poisoning isn't worth it.

Can I refreeze chicken that was thawed in the fridge?

Yes. If chicken was thawed in the refrigerator (not at room temperature or in warm water), it's safe to refreeze. However, the texture may be slightly degraded. If it was thawed using the cold water or microwave method, cook it before refreezing.

What temperature should chicken be cooked to?

Chicken should be cooked to an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) as measured with a food thermometer inserted into the thickest part. This kills Salmonella, Campylobacter, and other harmful bacteria.
